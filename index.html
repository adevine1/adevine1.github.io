Get Five!

Get Five! is a clone of a popular 5-dice game (i.e., Yahtzee) that I wrote in HTML/CSS/JS/JQuery. It is based on a hand-held device that I played quite a bit over the years. I made a clone of it a number of years ago for Windows (in Delphi), and this is a similar sort of project in JavaScript. Get Five! uses local storage to persist high-score data and user settings (i.e., sound effects on or off) between sessions.

Petals Around the Rose

Petals Around the Rose is an implementation of one of my favorite puzzle games. I wrote it in HTML/CSS/JQuery. It embodies a minimalist design, yet it is a good brain teaser for various levels of puzzle solving ability. Also, it's ephemeral: once it's solved, it cannot really be "played" again. I wrote it in HTML/CSS/JQuery. The rules of Petals Around the Rose are simple: guess the result of six dice that are rolled. The trick is that there is a specific way of calculating the total. To win the game, figure out how it is done. The only clue is the name of the game.

Simple Notes

Simple Notes is a very (very) simple place to jot notes, written in HTML/JS/CSS. It's a no-frills, not-meant-for-super-safe-keeping, one-font text editor. It's perfect for jotting something down quickly (say, while taking a phone call). It has one undo via a right-click with the mouse. It persists data on 'save' using local storage. (I keep it as the first upper-left bookmark on my bookmark bar, as it's quicker to access than other text programs I use. "Click-of-a-button accessible to jot down something not super-vital" is what I was going for.)
