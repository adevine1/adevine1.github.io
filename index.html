Get Five!

Get Five! is a clone of a popular 5-dice game (i.e., Yahtzee) that I wrote in HTML/CSS/JS/JQuery. It is based on a hand-held device that I've played quite a lot. I made a clone of it awhile ago for Windows (in Delphi), and I thought to try it again in JavaScript. Get Five! uses local storage to persist high-score data and user settings (i.e., sound effects on or off) between sessions.

Petals Around the Rose

Petals Around the Rose is an implementation of a puzzle game I am fond of. I like the puzzle because it embodies a minimalist design, yet it is a good brain teaser equally for varying levels of puzzle solving ability. Also, it's ephemeral: once it's solved, it cannot really be "played" again. I wrote it in HTML/CSS/JQuery. The rules of Petals Around the Rose are simple: guess the result of six dice that are rolled. The trick is that there is a specific way of calculating the total. To win the game, figure out how it is done. The only clue is the name of the game.

Simple Notes

Simple Notes is a very (very) simple place to jot notes, written in HTML/JS/CSS. It's a no-frills, one-font text editor. It's for jotting something down quickly (say, while taking a phone call). It persists data using local storage. (I keep it as the left-most bookmark on my bookmark bar, as it's quicker to access than other text programs I use. "Click-of-a-button accessible to jot down something not super-vital" is what I was going for.)
